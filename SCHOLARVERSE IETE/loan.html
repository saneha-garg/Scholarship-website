FAQs regarding Monetary Aids




Q- I am interested in attending school and obtaining financial aid. How do I apply for Federal financial aid?

A- You must complete a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) for each year you are in school, reporting your assets and income, as well as your parents' assets and income if you are considered a dependent student. See http://www.fafsa.ed.gov/ for the FAFSA application and what information is required to complete it.
For more information about Federal student financial aid, including grants, loans, and scholarships, as well as private sources of aid, see:
https://www.lawhelp.org/dc/resource/frequently-asked-questions-about-student-loan

Q- Who is eligible for Education Loan in India?How should I proceed?

A- Anyone who is citizen of India is eligible for the loan.
For more information visit:
http://www.studyguideindia.com/Education-Loan/Education-loan-India-Faq.asp

Q- Should I apply for scholarships, loans, or grants first?

A- The best strategy is to first apply to all grant and scholarship opportunities that you are eligible for and only then apply for loans to cover the deficit.
For more information visit:
https://www.collegeraptor.com/paying-for-college/articles/student-loans/faqs-student-loans/

Q- What are some loan schemes run by Indian Government?

A- A fully IT based Student Financial Aid Authority has been proposed through the 'Pradhan Mantri Vidya Lakshmi Karyakram', to administer and monitor Scholarship as well Educational Loan Schemes, with a view to enable all poor and middle class students to pursue higher education of their choice without any constraint of funds.
Visit:
https://www.india.gov.in/spotlight/pradhan-mantri-vidya-lakshmi-karyakram-towards-bright-future
